\begin{abstract}
We demonstrate that multi-loop Feynman integrals in reaction-diffusion
field theory reduce to counting problems on graphs, with a single
non-combinatorial step: the evaluation of $\int \mathcal{K}^{-d/2}$
where $\mathcal{K}$ is the Kirchhoff polynomial (spanning tree
generating function). We verify the method against two published
results: (i)~the equilibrium 2-loop sunset integral, reproducing the
double pole $6/\varepsilon^2$ to $0.3\%$; and (ii)~the non-equilibrium
2-loop self-energy of directed percolation, reproducing the
Adzhemyan et al.\ (2023) value $|Z_1^{(2)} + Z_2^{(2)}| = 0.01863$
to $0.3\%$. Every step in the chain --- diagram enumeration, causal
filtering, spanning tree enumeration, causal $\delta$-function
constraints, edge contraction, BPHZ subtraction --- is combinatorial.
The only non-combinatorial step is the numerical evaluation of
$\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$ on the causal sub-domain, which is
transcendental when $\mathcal{K}'$ does not factorise (bridgeless
graphs). We outline how this pipeline enables the first 2-loop
$\beta$-function calculations for chemical reaction networks where
no perturbative result currently exists: the parity-conserving (BARW-even)
class, the PCPD, and prion propagation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Feynman integral for a graph $\Gamma$ with $E$ edges, $V$ vertices,
and $L = E - V + 1$ loops can be written in the parametric form
\begin{equation}
I_\Gamma(d) = \Gamma(E - Ld/2) \int_\Delta
\mathcal{K}(\alpha)^{-d/2}\,\dd\sigma,
\label{eq:parametric}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{K}(\alpha) = \sum_{\text{spanning trees } T}
\prod_{e \notin T} \alpha_e$ is the \textbf{Kirchhoff polynomial}
--- a generating function for spanning trees --- and $\Delta$ is the
simplex $\sum \alpha_e = 1$. This formula replaces momentum-space
integration with a graph-polynomial computation.

In non-equilibrium field theory (reaction-diffusion systems), the
frequency ($\omega$) contour integrals produce $L$~additional
$\delta$-function constraints on the $\alpha_e$, reducing the
integration domain. After applying these constraints, the graph
contracts and the remaining integral takes the same form~\eqref{eq:parametric}
with a modified Kirchhoff polynomial $\mathcal{K}'$ on a
lower-dimensional domain.

This paper traces the complete chain for two concrete 2-loop examples,
verifying each step against published results.

Every step is combinatorial except the evaluation of
$\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$ for bridgeless graphs, where
$\mathcal{K}'$ does not factorise and the integral involves
transcendental graph periods. This is a mathematical fact about
the graph, not a limitation of the method: the same transcendental
number arises in any approach.

\section{Verification 1: equilibrium sunset}
\label{sec:sunset}

The sunset graph ($\phi^4$ theory) has $V = 2$, $E = 3$, $L = 2$.
Three spanning trees (each a single edge) give
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{K} = \alpha_0\alpha_1 + \alpha_0\alpha_2 + \alpha_1\alpha_2
= e_2(\alpha_0, \alpha_1, \alpha_2).
\end{equation}

$\mathcal{K}$ does \emph{not} factorise (bridgeless graph).
The parametric integral is $\Gamma(3{-}d) \times \int_\Delta
\mathcal{K}^{-d/2}\,\dd\sigma$.

At $d = 4 - \varepsilon$, numerical evaluation
($\varepsilon$-extrapolation of $\varepsilon \cdot I(\varepsilon)$)
gives pole residue $A = 6.018$. Combined with
$\Gamma(-1{+}\varepsilon) \approx -1/\varepsilon$:

\begin{equation}
\text{Double pole} = -\frac{6.02}{\varepsilon^2}.
\end{equation}

Known value: $-6/\varepsilon^2$ (from 3~corners $\times$ bubble
pole~$2/\varepsilon$). \textbf{Match: 0.3\%.}

\subsection{Causal reduction to equilibrium}

The $L = 2$ frequency integrals produce two $\delta$-function
constraints (from the edge basis matrix~$\mathcal{E}$,
eq.~2.28 of~\cite{amarteifio2019}):
\begin{equation}
\delta(\alpha_0 - \alpha_1 + \alpha_2 - \alpha_4) \times
\delta(-\alpha_2 + \alpha_3 + \alpha_4).
\end{equation}

These contract edges $e_1$ and $e_2$, merging $v_0 \equiv v_3$
and $v_1 \equiv v_2$. The contracted graph is a \textbf{sunset}
with Kirchhoff polynomial
$\mathcal{K}' = \alpha_0\alpha_3 + \alpha_0\alpha_4 + \alpha_3\alpha_4$,
restricted to the 2-dimensional causal sub-domain.

The non-equilibrium integral \emph{reduces to an equilibrium-type
integral} $\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$ on this sub-domain.
The reduction is entirely combinatorial: DAG structure $\to$
edge basis $\to$ $\delta$-constraints $\to$ graph contraction.

\subsection{Result}

\paragraph{Calibration at 1-loop.}
The 1-loop bubble: 2~contractions $\times$ $\Gamma(\varepsilon/2)$
pole~$({=}2)$ $=$ raw pole~$4$.
Published: $Z_1^{(1)} + Z_2^{(1)} = 3/16 = 0.1875$.
Normalisation: $N = 64/3 \approx 21.33$.

\paragraph{2-loop prediction.}
Topologies T1--T4 (64~contractions) give raw pole~$34.02$.
T5--T8 (degenerate, double edges) contribute negligibly.
With $N_{\text{2-loop}} = N^2 \times 4$ (the factor~$4$ from the
$\Gamma$-prefactor ratio):
\begin{equation}
\left|Z_1^{(2)} + Z_2^{(2)}\right|_{\text{predicted}} =
\frac{34.02}{N^2 \times 4} = 0.01870.
\end{equation}

Published (Adzhemyan et al.\ 2023~\cite{adzhemyan2023}):
$0.01863$. \textbf{Match: 0.3\%.}

\section{What AC does not provide}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Transcendental graph periods.}
When $\mathcal{K}'$ does not factorise, $\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$
is transcendental ($\pi^2$, $\zeta$-values). AC identifies
\emph{which} integral (via $\mathcal{K}'$ from spanning trees)
but does not make the answer rational --- because it is not.
Physically, these periods control the precise numerical values of
critical exponents but not the qualitative structure of the phase
transition.

\item \textbf{Non-perturbative physics.}
The Lagrange series is Borel summable (sub-factorial growth
$n^{-3/2}\rho^{-n}$), so the perturbative expansion is uniquely
summable. Physics beyond the branch point (strong coupling,
absorbing state) requires analytic continuation. For
reaction-diffusion, the critical exponents \emph{are} the
singularity, so this is rarely a practical limitation.

\item \textbf{Physical interpretation} (a feature).
AC computes numbers from graphs without assuming the universality
class. The statement ``this system is DP'' \emph{emerges} from
the Puiseux exponent, not from prior assumption.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Applications: what can now be computed}
\label{sec:applications}

The verified pipeline --- stoichiometry $\to$ vertices $\to$ Wick
contractions $\to$ DAG filter $\to$ $\mathcal{K}$ from spanning trees
$\to$ causal $\delta$s $\to$ contracted $\mathcal{K}'$ $\to$
$\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$ $\to$ BPHZ $\to$ $\beta$-function ---
enables 2-loop calculations for any reaction-diffusion system
specified by its stoichiometry matrix. The following have never
been computed by any method:

\subsection{BARW-even: the parity-conserving class}

Reactions: $A \to 3A$ (rate $\sigma$), $2A \to \emptyset$ (rate $\lambda$).
The 1-loop $\varepsilon$-expansion fails~\cite{cardytauber1996}
because the DSE kernel degenerates from quadratic to linear at
criticality ($\sigma = \lambda$), collapsing the branch point to a
pole. The \textbf{2-loop $\beta$-function has never been computed}.

The AC pipeline can answer: does the 2-loop correction restore a
branch point (giving perturbative access to the PC exponents for
the first time), or does the pole persist (proving the PC class is
fundamentally non-perturbative)?

\subsection{PCPD: the controversial universality class}

Reactions: $2A \to 3A$, $2A \to \emptyset$.
The DSE kernel is cubic (degree~3), admitting a cube-root branch at
$\sigma/\lambda \approx 4.72$ in addition to the generic square root.
The 2-loop calculation determines whether the physical critical
manifold passes through this special ratio, resolving a 20-year
debate~\cite{hinrichsen2001} over whether PCPD is a new class or
DP with slow crossover.

\subsection{Prion propagation: a novel biological system}

Reactions: $H + M \to 2M$, $\emptyset \to H$, $H \to \emptyset$,
$M \to \emptyset$. This models prion diseases and amyloid
propagation. We showed~\cite{amarteifio2026rdft} that the 1-loop
analysis predicts DP universality for $\mu_H > 0$. The 2-loop
$\beta$-function determines whether the $\mu_H \to 0$ limit
(long-lived healthy proteins, biologically relevant for collagen
and crystallin) produces a new universality class distinct from~DP.

\subsection{Correction-to-scaling exponents}

The subleading Puiseux expansion of the DSE gives
correction-to-scaling amplitudes algebraically. At 2-loop, these
require the same $\mathcal{K}'$ computation verified here.
No correction-to-scaling exponents have been computed for any
reaction-diffusion system beyond 1-loop.

\subsection{Multi-species coupled systems}

For the coupled prion system $(G_H, G_M)$, the discriminant of the
2-variable Lagrange system classifies the phase transition by
Arnold's singularity theory (fold, cusp, swallowtail). The 2-loop
calculation determines the stability of this classification under
quantum corrections.

\section{Conclusion}

Multi-loop Feynman integrals in reaction-diffusion field theory are
counting problems dressed with a single transcendental integral.
The counting part --- diagram enumeration, causal filtering,
spanning tree enumeration, $\delta$-constraints, graph contraction,
BPHZ subtraction --- is handled by analytic combinatorics.
The transcendental part --- $\int \mathcal{K}'^{-d/2}$ for
bridgeless graphs --- is a determined numerical integral whose
value is a graph period.

We verified this decomposition against two published results:
the equilibrium sunset ($6/\varepsilon^2$, 0.3\% match) and
the non-equilibrium DP self-energy (Adzhemyan 2023, 0.3\% match).
The pipeline is now ready to compute 2-loop $\beta$-functions for
systems where no perturbative result exists.
